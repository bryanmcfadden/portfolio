Bulleted

Overview

Bulleted lists can be effective in helping the reader understand the organization of key points, but be sure to use them judiciously. Use bulleted lists to draw attention to key information that would get lost with a paragraph of text.

Content

  • Limit the number of bulleted items to those that are completely necessary.
  • Although there is not specific character limit, bulleted items should be concise. Avoid paragraphs of text.

Special Considerations

Avoid making bulleted list items overly complex - such as including images or tables or other formatted elements nested within a bullet item.

Limit the use of nested bullet items.

Numbered

Overview

Numbered lists can be used within the body to denote items in a series, such as procedural steps. Numbered lists stand out from the regular text and can add clarity to the text.
Numbered lists can be useful to show the relationship between items: a chronology of events, each item’s relative importance, and so on.
The items can be sentences, phrases or full paragraphs. The first words of the sentences are capitalized and appropriate end punctuation should be used.

Content

  • Use at least two items within a numbered list.
  • For a single step, use a bullet.
  • Limit the number items to those that are completely necessary.
  • Although there is not a specific character limit, numbered items should be concise. Avoid paragraphs of text.

Special Considerations

Avoid making numbered list items overly complex - such as including images, tables or other formatted elements nested within a numbered item.
