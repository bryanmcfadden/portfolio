Color Palette

As a reference, these are the AT&T brand colors represented in hex and RGB values. The primary palette consists of three colors: AT&T Orange, AT&T Blue and white. White is used as a background color to provide good contrast against the vibrant colors in our palette. AT&T Gray Highlight may also be used as a background in a header or description box. Do not use any other brand colors for backgrounds within content areas, otherwise text legibility may be compromised.

Layout

A layout determines the arrangement of all the components or content types in a page. Users will find what they want quickly in a well-designed layout; consequently, a better user experience will be provided. A well-designed layout has:

  • A focal point that users’ eyes are immediately drawn to
  • A natural path or arrangement the users’ eyes can follow
  • Logical grouping of elements
  • Consistent alignment of components to promote an organized view
  • Just the right amount of content and white space; it makes effective use of the screen real estate

Some of the most common layouts used in TDC are two and three column layouts.

Two Column Layout

This is a very simple layout typically used when you have two types of content. In TDC, you’ll generally see a column dedicated to announcements, news, or events, and another used for main content. If you have content that you can categorize into any of the aforementioned sections then you should use this layout.

Please note that it is acceptable to have more than one section in one column; so if you wanted to have a column with any and all kinds of news you could have one section for “Latest News” and another with “Announcements” beneath it. This kind of arrangement can be seen in the leftmost column in the TDC homepage.

Three Column Layout

This layout is commonly seen in the CSI API pages. If you have several pages you want users to access, then you will need a menu. If the content within those pages is split in various sections then you will also need a table of contents.

TDC uses a three-column layout with a menu, main content section, and table of contents; this layout also includes two portlets in the middle column where you can add links to news or events, general quick links, and/or define terms you’ve used in the main content.
